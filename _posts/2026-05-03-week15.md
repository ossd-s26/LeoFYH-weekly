**Refection**
This week’s class was really interesting because we got to see how different groups approached open source work and presentations. It made me realize how much progress everyone has made, especially when it comes to reading code and actually contributing to real projects.
For our group, we continued working on our project and thinking about how to improve both functionality and collaboration. One thing I noticed is that group work becomes much smoother when everyone clearly understands the codebase. Compared to earlier in the semester, we’re now much faster at navigating files, identifying issues, and discussing solutions. AI tools also played a big role here—they helped us quickly understand unfamiliar parts of the code and test ideas, which made the whole process more efficient.
In terms of presentations, I saw a group talk about their contributions to a p5.js project. It was impressive because they didn’t just explore the code—they actually fixed multiple bugs. Seeing real bug fixes made their work feel impactful and practical. Another group presented their contribution to an open source live streaming software project. One feature they worked on was enabling video playback in the background, which is a small but very useful improvement for user experience. It showed how even focused contributions can make a meaningful difference.
Overall, I am pretty happy about what I've learnt this semester!
