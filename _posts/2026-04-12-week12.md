**Week 12 Reflection**
In class, we discussed how companies use and participate in open source, and I found it very interesting that open source itself is not a business model, but companies can build business models around it.

Many companies use open source because it allows faster innovation and lower development cost. Since the code is shared, developers from around the world can contribute and improve the software together. This creates a strong community, which is very valuable for both users and companies.

However, companies still need to make money. The PPT showed several common business models. One is support and services, where companies provide installation, maintenance, or training. Another is open core, where the basic version is free but advanced features are paid. There is also SaaS, where users pay for online services.

In terms of participation, companies contribute to open source by assigning developers, funding projects, or maintaining tools. This helps them build reputation, attract talent, and influence the direction of the technology.
