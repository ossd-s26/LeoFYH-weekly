**Week 11 Reflection**
The cathedral model means a small team builds software slowly and carefully. Everything is planned, and changes are controlled. The bazaar model is open and fast. Many people can join, test, and improve the code together.

I personally like the bazaar model more. It feels more creative and flexible. When more people give ideas, the project can grow faster and become better. This is similar to how games improve through player feedback.

But AI tools change this idea a lot. Now, even one developer can work like a “team” using AI. AI can help write code, fix bugs, and give suggestions quickly. This makes the cathedral model faster than before, because small teams can still move quickly.

At the same time, AI also makes the bazaar model stronger. More people can join coding without deep knowledge, since AI helps them. This lowers the barrier to entry.

In my opinion, AI is mixing these two models. A small team can move fast like a bazaar, while still keeping control like a cathedral. In the future, development may not be one or the other, but a combination of both.

We are still looking for open issues and submitting PRs to fix them. Nanobot is a really cool agent, similar to OpenClaw, but it still has some bugs. While fixing issues, we also noticed other problems, such as misleading text like “finished” being shown when the task is still executing.

I also made another OpenStreetMap edit ([changeset #180877384](https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/180877384)) to fix the direction of a pedestrian-crossing sign near Broadway, 17th Avenue, and Lincoln Street in Denver, so routing and map display match the real street layout.

Our group and other groups are making similar progress. We have all identified the main logic and functionality of the project. Now, the focus is on optimizing it by fixing minor bugs.
